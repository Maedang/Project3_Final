Our Purpose

For our project, we want to differentiate and compare death risk factors between ten countries that we handpicked from around the world. The countries include Netherlands, USA, Mexico, Australia, Japan, Brazil, England, Korea, Ghana, and Morocco.

We looked through the data that we chose to use for this project and chose the most common and highest death risk factors of countries. The death risk factors that we chose include high blood pressure, smoking, alcohol use, high body-mass index (Obesity), and drug use.

We want to figure out which one of these death risk factors causes the most death out of all the countries we chose from. We want to then see how these risk factors fluctuate throughout multiple years and different parts of the world. We made sure to choose countries that can account for almost every region in the world to get a better measurement of the risk factors. Another key thing we want to look for within the data is how different age groups are affected by the risk factors and if they fluctuate between countries. For example, does the age group of 50 to 90 years old get affected by Obesity the same in the United States and in Korea.
